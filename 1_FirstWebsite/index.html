Wave Particle Duality

An important concept when studying subatomic particles is the wave particle duality. This means that their behavior is both that of a wave and that of a particle. This is a little bit hard to cenceptualize, but we now have a lot of analogies to do so. Imagine this as a single wave of visible light (a photon with a wavelength of about 500 nanometers), when this wave impacts a surface it does so in a single point even though it's actually a wave. It's like a ship that creates waves, but when they reach the shore it turns out to only appear in a single spot.

Fermions

This are a special subset of subatomic particles and are known as matter particles. This subset is divided into Quarks and Leptons.

Bosons

Another special subset of subatomic particles and they're known as force particles. This subset is responsible of the interactions between fermions; they are responsible for electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions.

Entanglement & Superposition

This topics might be a little bit above the scope of this site, but I think they're cool. Entanglement is a concept of quantum mechanics that represents a "link" between two or more particles. Superposition is the state of a particle and this state is "unknown" until we observe the particle. To link this two concepts let's use a metafor: imagine you order two pizzas, one cheese and the other pepperoni, when the boxes are closed you don't know which one's which and thus we could say they're in superposition, the interesting thing is that the moment we open one box (observe it) we immediately know what's inside of the other box. In this simple metaphor we have entanglement between the boxes because by knowing the state of one we know the state of the other and this same thing happens between particles that might be zillions of miles appart.
